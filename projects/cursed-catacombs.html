Cursed Catacombs

Playable · Godot 4.7

A turn-based tactical deckbuilder on a 5x5 grid — think Into the Breach's positioning crossed with Slay the Spire's deck-building, in a single self-contained run rather than a sprawling meta-game. Every fight is won or lost on where your Hero is standing as much as which card you play.

Core loop

  • A run is 5 encounters, fought one at a time on a walled 5x5 stone room. Your Hero and the enemy roster occupy tiles; every card either attacks, defends, moves, or does something stranger, targeting a specific pattern of tiles relative to whoever plays it — Strike only reaches an adjacent enemy, Reckless Strike only reaches exactly two tiles out, so range and positioning are part of the decision, not just the card itself.
  • Cards cost action points, 2 per turn to spend however you like: two 1-cost cards, or one 2-cost card, or a 1-cost card and pass. A cheap Strike-Strike turn out-damages a single Heavy Strike, but the Heavy Strike lands it all in one hit — the deck is full of trade-offs like that rather than strict upgrades.
  • Enemies are distinct problems, not palette swaps. Skeletons are a straightforward melee race. Slimes ignore block entirely, so Guard is dead weight against them. Archers kite — they'll retreat rather than let you close to melee range, and hit you from two tiles out if you don't. Bone Guards regenerate their own block every turn, so chipping away slowly doesn't work; you either commit a big hit or clear their block outright with Sunder.
  • HP carries across all 5 encounters in a run — damage from an early fight is still with you going into the escalated final encounter, so a between-fight reward screen lets you add a new card to your deck, rest and heal, or skip both and keep your deck lean.
  • Death ends the run and resets your deck and HP back to the starting Strike/Lunge/Guard baseline for next time — but any reward card you were ever offered stays permanently unlocked (check the in-game Codex), so later runs draw from a wider pool even after a loss.
