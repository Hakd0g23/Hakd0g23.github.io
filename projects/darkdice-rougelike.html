darkdice-rougelike

Playable · Godot 4.7 (Web Build)

A roguelike deckbuilder — but with dice. Every round is a poker-dice hand instead of a card hand: roll a pool of d6s (and eventually bigger dice), lock the ones worth keeping, reroll the rest, and submit for a chips-x-mult score against that round's target. Clear enough rounds and gold buys permanent-for-the-run relics and pool upgrades between hands — the loop will feel immediately familiar if you've played Balatro, reskinned around dice instead of cards.

Core loop

  • Roll, lock, reroll, submit. Each hand starts with a full roll of your dice pool (5 dice to start). Lock any dice you want to keep, spend up to 3 rerolls on the rest, then submit. The best-scoring combo the pool can form — High Die up through Two Pair, Three-of-a-Kind, Straight, Full House, and all the way to Eight-of-a-Kind — sets the chips and mult for that hand.
  • Combos level up as you play them, permanently for the run, the same way Balatro hands level up — lean on the combo you're building toward and it keeps paying off better.
  • Beat the round's target score within a limited number of hands (4 to start) to clear it and bank gold — clearing with hands to spare pays a bonus.
  • Spend gold in the shop between rounds on Hexes (passive relics — the Joker equivalent — that trigger off dice-specific conditions, like Serpent's Eyes doubling Mult on a pair of 1s, or Colossus rewarding a big Six-of-a-Kind-or-better pool), Dice Enhancements (Glass dice that shatter for a Mult bonus, Cursed dice that roll low but pay gold, Wild dice that reinforce whatever face is already winning), or pool changes — add a die, remove one, or upgrade a die's sides up the chain from d6 to d8, d12, d20 for a wider range and a higher ceiling.
  • Every third round is a Boss Wager — a one-round twist like no locking, no rerolls, odd dice scoring zero, or gold cut in half — that forces you to actually use whatever Hexes and enhancements you've bought to counter it.

Progress within a run — round, gold, owned Hexes, dice pool, combo levels — autosaves after every action, so you can close the tab mid-hand and pick the run back up later; dying resets the run back to round one. One deliberate accessibility call in the UI: die color is reserved to mean "this specific die," never a state indicator — locked and enhanced dice are marked with a border ring and a corner badge instead of a recolor, so state reads the same regardless of color vision.
